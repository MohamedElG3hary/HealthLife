What is the first aid?

First aid refers to medical attention that is usually administered immediately after the injury occurs and at the location where it occurred and immediate and effective first aid interventions can significantly improve survival rates in various emergencies including cardiac arrest , choking incidents and severe injuries.

meaning of ABCs of first aid

Airway » if someone is not breathing Clear their airway.
Breathing » if the airway is clear and they are still not breathing Provid rescue breathing.
Circulation » perform chest compressions to keep blood circulating as well as rescue breathing if the person is breathing but unresponsive Check their pulse if their heart has stopped provide chest compressions.

CPR ‹ cardiopulmonary resuctiation ›

A few of injuries you may face:
Diagnosis Symptoms Degree of risk First aid steps
wound bleeding depends on the wound depth Make an effort to stop bleeding by applying direct pressure to the wound, keeping the pressure steady with bandages, and raising the injured limb above the heart level if possible
choke
  • Skin, lips and nails that change color turning blue or gray
  • Inability to talk
  • Strained or noisy breathing
 hazardous Do CPR Techniques
minor burns
  • it hurts
  • not deep
  • skin looks dry and red
 minimal Apply a clean towel soaked with cold water to burn to relieve pain and then cover the area with a clean bandage. Roll the burn without tightening the ligament on it to avoid pressure on the burned skin
deep burns
  • painful
  • deep
  • skin looks white and stiff
 hazardous Cover loosely with sterile, nonstick bandage or, for large areas, a sheet or other material that won't leave lint in wound and call an ambulance as soon as possible
bone fractures
  • curvature, swelling of the area
  • inability to move
 average Apply ice packs to limit swelling and help relieve pain , Immobilise the broken bone by placing a padded splint along the injured limb Secure the splint by passing the bandages above and below the break
First aid is not enough. You must call emergency services 123 for professional medical assistance
